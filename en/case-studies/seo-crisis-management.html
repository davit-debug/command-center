When your business relies almost entirely on Google as its primary source of leads, losing search engine visibility isn't just a technical problem—it's a direct hit to your revenue. This is especially true in a niche industry where advertising on platforms like Facebook is simply ineffective.

This was the exact situation our client found themselves in—a B2B company offering highly specialized consulting services in the occupational safety sector.

Step One — Identifying the Problem

We began with a detailed SEO audit to determine why the client's service page wasn't appearing for their primary lead-generating keyword. We discovered the page had a Google "penalty"—an old technical error caused the system to devalue the page, completely excluding it from search results.

Interestingly, a less thematically relevant blog post was still ranking in the SERPs, but only on the 3rd page, which generated virtually no traffic.

"Our main service page completely disappeared from Google. The leads stopped. We needed a fast solution."

— Company Director

The Technical Fix That Changed Everything

First, we overhauled the URL structure, changing the service page's address to align it perfectly with the target keyword theme. At the same time, we updated the meta titles and descriptions.

Next, we implemented a 301 redirect: the old, ranking blog post was redirected to the service page to properly consolidate and transfer its content authority.

We implemented an internal linking strategy, adding links to the service page from other relevant blog posts. We also secured one high-quality backlink from a thematically relevant and authoritative source.

Google's First Green Light

Within days of our technical intervention, the first positive signals appeared. Google began to re-index the page, and soon after, it appeared in search results for the first time at position #10. This was a turning point: 10xSEO brought a page that was previously invisible back into the game.

The Result: Top 3 Position in 2 Months

Two months later, the page secured its place where it matters most: the third position for the keyword that directly generates leads for the client.

This is just the beginning. Our strategy is designed not only to maintain this position but to improve it. Our next goal is already set: to claim the #1 spot for the client's most commercially valuable keyword.
